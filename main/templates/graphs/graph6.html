Analysis of Economic Disparities Across Social Groups

Objective: This analysis aims to highlight economic disparities among different social groups in both urban and rural settings, providing insights into spending patterns and socio-economic challenges. Understanding these gaps is essential for designing effective policies to promote inclusive economic growth.

Key Insights

Urban-Rural Gap

Urban households have significantly higher per capita expenditure than rural households, reflecting better economic conditions and financial opportunities

Challenges Across Social Groups

Scheduled Tribes (ST) and Scheduled Castes (SC) have the lowest spending capacity, while Other Backward Classes (OBC) and the ‘Others’ category fare better. In urban areas, the ‘Others’ group records an average Monthly Per Capita Expenditure (MPCE) of ₹7,333.

Rural Hardships & Urban Advantage

Scheduled Tribes in rural areas have the lowest MPCE at ₹3,016, highlighting severe economic challenges. Even weaker sections in urban areas, like SC households with ₹5,307 MPCE, have higher spending power than the most stable rural groups, reinforcing urban economic benefits.

Solutions

Education & Digital Access

Strengthen rural education with better infrastructure, quality teachers, and financial aid. Promote adult literacy and digital education to enhance financial and technological skills, with a focus on empowering women through education.

Infrastructure, Connectivity & Agriculture

Improve housing, sanitation, roads, transport, and internet access while ensuring 100% electrification and clean water. Support farmers with equipment-sharing, technology-driven agriculture, and agri-tourism to boost productivity and incomes.

Governance & Community Development

Strengthen self-help groups and Gram Panchayats for local development, ensure transparency in welfare programs, and promote corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives for sustainable rural progress.
